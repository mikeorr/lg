John -

I don't know that there's such a thing as "the best" in this regard (although there are several books I can think of that are contending for the title of "worst".) Migrating from Wind0ws does not normally require books, although it may, in complex cases, require the help of a friendly experienced Linux user such as may be found at your local Linux Users Group (LUG). In the far more common, simple situations, I recommend simply popping in a LiveCD distribution - e.g., Knoppix (http://www.knopper.net/knoppix/index-en.html) and using it for a while. If you run into any problems, you can usually get answers here, or from the help documentation that comes with Knoppix, or in a number of places on the Web. "Migration", unless you're a large company with a number of complex services, is mostly an imaginary construct that just gets in the way.

Give it a shot, and let us know how it goes!
